Photonic or Optical Computing

Optical or photonic computing uses photons produced by lasers or diodes for computation. Currently, computers use semi conductor based electronic parts or integrated circuits. Last year, scientists were able to make a huge step forward; for the first time ever, they were able to store optical based information as sound waves on a computer chip.

Think of fibre optics - an internet connection using light to transmit information. Well, currently our computers are unable to receive that information as light directly, thus needing to change it, slow it down to electric information before it is processed. This is not only a huge decrease in performance, but efficiency as well.

Photonic computing advantages:

  • Potential to run at minimum 20x faster than current laptops/computers
  • Less heat generated in the parts, increasing efficiency and reducing wear on parts
  • Less energy usage

Photonic computing disadvantages:

  • Information stored as photons, too fast for existing microchips to read

ScienceAlert: FIONA MACDONALD

New Breakthrough:

Scientists as recently as last year were able to slow down the information stored as photons and convert it into sound. Photons, which might have passed through the chip in 2-3ms, now is slow enough as sound to be processed by new microchips that are being tested. "The information in our chip in acoustic form travels at a velocity five orders of magnitude slower than in the optical domain," said project supervisor Birgit Stiller from University of Sydney in Australia. Team member Moritz Merklein added: "For [light-based computers] to become a commercial reality, photonic data on the chip needs to be slowed down so that they can be processed, routed, stored and accessed."

The team did this by developing a memory system that accurately transfers between light and sound waves on a photonic microchip - the kind of chip that will be used in light-based computers. The fact that the team was able to convert the light into sound waves not only slowed it down, but also made data retrieval more accurate.
